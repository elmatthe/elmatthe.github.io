## About Elijah Matthew

I'm a finance graduate with a focus on wealth management and financial planning, backed by a solid foundation in investment analysis and business law. I'm particularly interested in implementing strong process, clear documentation, and thoughtful portfolio construction to make advice more consistent and easier for anyone, regardless of financial expertise, to understand.

My goal is to bring together technical execution and straightforward communication: taking markets, tax rules, and product complexity and turning them into simple, practical recommendations that fit each client's goals and constraints. I enjoy improving workflows, tightening up data, and using automation to remove repetitive work from planning and review.

I've had exposure to private wealth, tax preparation, and community leadership, which has given me experience working with clients, colleagues, and stakeholders in different settings. I'm comfortable with tools like Excel, Bloomberg Terminal, and Power BI, and I use Python and VBA to build utilities, checks, and templates that make planning, monitoring, and reporting more efficient.

### Core Interests
- Building and documenting investment policy and strategic asset allocation that ties back to real-world client objectives.
- Maintaining rebalancing discipline and risk management through clear rules and monitoring, not one-off decisions.
- Making tax-aware planning and account structure choices across registered, corporate, and non-registered accounts.
- Using automation (Python, VBA, and advanced Excel) to streamline planning workflows, review checklists, and advisor productivity tools.
- Applying data and technology, including web scraping, Power BI, and firm systems, to support better and faster decision-making.
- Communicating in plain language so clients and teammates can quickly see what matters and why.

### Beyond Finance
A big part of my background comes from playing high-level competitive hockey in Edmonton, including U18 AAA and elite minor hockey. I've been in environments where discipline, preparation, and quick decision-making were non-negotiable. I've also worked on the other side of the game as a referee and power skating instructor, which sharpened my communication, leadership, and conflict-management skills.

Outside of work and hockey, I play guitar and enjoy the slow, iterative process of getting better: learning new progressions, breaking down songs, and focusing on small improvements over time. That same mindset of consistent practice and feedback is how I approach learning new tools, building better workflows, and developing as an advisor.

### Professional Direction
This repository is meant to function as my advisory toolkit: a collection of projects, templates, and notes that reflect how I think about real planning and review work. It includes examples of workflows, investment and policy frameworks, and small automation projects aimed at making advisory processes more structured and repeatable.

I'm building my career in wealth management and financial planning, and I'm currently working through credentials such as the Canadian Securities Course and the Certified Financial Planner designation. I'm especially interested in roles where I can support portfolio managers and advisors by combining analysis, process design, and practical tools to help them serve clients more effectively.
